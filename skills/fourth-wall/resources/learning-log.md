## 2026-07-13 - Complete-slice delegation
- Status: promoted
- Scope: cost | coordination | proof
- Observation: orchestrated roots retained implementation, integration, polling, and live-proof loops after dispatch, causing roots to consume 49% of the comparison proxy versus 42% for children.
- Evidence: 10 roots, 72 subagents, 4,325 root inferences versus 3,541 child inferences; the CSR booking root reached 180K input context, five compactions, and 1,107 root inferences while its 16 children used 529.
- Confidence: high
- Proposed change: delegate coherent implementation-through-proof slices, stabilized integration loops, and runtime proof while retaining Git authority and final acceptance at the root.

## 2026-07-15 - Capability floors and asymmetric cooldown
- Status: superseded by the 2026-07-22 ownership-preserving routing policy
- Scope: routing | context | proof
- Observation: a dispatch regression campaign began with scoped and standard work but required specialized and critical owners for cross-boundary Rinf architecture, runtime event amplification, native multi-window lifecycle, and macOS accessibility crash acceptance. Lower-effort routes produced rework while verbose builds and repeated handoffs amplified context cost.
- Evidence: the CSR dispatch campaign reached independent fresh-stack and platform acceptance only after task-family escalation; owner review compared GPT-5.6 coding, specialized, and 256K-512K long-context results with model pricing and accepted the revised routing table.
- Confidence: high
- Proposed change: raise every tier's reasoning floor, treat Terra as long-context insurance, escalate promptly by risk signature, cool down one dimension only after two three-success acceptance windows, and keep durable routing state in a compact `/tmp` task packet.

## 2026-07-22 - Ownership-preserving implementation and review
- Status: promoted
- Scope: routing | lifecycle | cost | proof
- Observation: Sol-heavy implementation graphs still required adversarial corrections, while bounded implementation slices generally succeeded once architecture, ownership, and proof were explicit. Most campaign friction came from late contract discovery, runtime environments, cold builds, and repeated handoffs rather than inability to implement a clear contract. Reviewers added the most value by finding security, provider-state, lifecycle, and architectural defects, not by repeating Worker validation.
- Evidence: matched-window analysis found Workers represented about 36% of cache-adjusted workload while Luna and Terra together handled about 1.4% of calls; review of six recent orchestration campaigns found strong Luna candidates in pricing, package-builder, Container Lab guidance, reviewer-directed repairs, tests, and platform cleanup. Current MultiAgentV2 source confirms `followup_task` preserves an existing child but exposes no model or reasoning override.
- Confidence: high
- Proposed change: use Terra for broad mapping, Luna high as the default explicit implementation owner, and Sol for design and adversarial judgment; return bounded review findings to the same Worker, avoid duplicate validation, classify attributable rework separately from adjacent healing and contract discovery, escalate reasoning gradually with recorded reasons, and use a forked successor only while native reactivation cannot alter capability.
